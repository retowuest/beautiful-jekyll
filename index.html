I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Political Science and International Relations at the University of Geneva. After predoctoral research stays in the Department of Politics at Princeton University and the Department of Politics at New York University, I received my PhD from the University of Geneva in 2016.

My primary research interests include legislative behavior, political representation, and data science. In my dissertation, I developed game-theoretical and empirical models to explore the reasons for and consequences of roll-call voting in legislatures. To read more about my work, see the Projects dropdown menu. Please feel free to contact me for more information.
